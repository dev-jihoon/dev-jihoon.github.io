“Break a Leg!” - Where did it come from? and why do people say ‘break a leg’?
What is the meaning of break a leg’?
If you’re a non-native English speaker, this phrase must sound pretty strange.
Does it mean that people wish harm on others?
No, not at all! That’s not what it means.
It’s just another strange, and wonderful English idiom.
This phrase is actually used to with people, especially actors good luck before a performance.
‘Break a leg!’ is an English expression which is used in the context of theatre.
This phrase, which means ‘Good luck’, is usually said to actors on stage.
Although it may sound strange, this phrase has been used by many people.
now, people say “Break a leg!” outside of the theatre situation.
Where did the phrase come from?
Here is a popular story.
People say that is originated in the early 20th century, in the American theatre community.
People believed that if they wished an actor ‘good luck,’ it would actually bring the actor bad luck.
So they told the actor to ‘break a leg’, hoping that the actor would show a perfect performance on stage.
That was a good thing which could happen to the actor.
